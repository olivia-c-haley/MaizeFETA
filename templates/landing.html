About FETA

FETA (Functional Enrichment Term Analysis) is a web-based platform developed to support functional genomics research in Zea mays. FETA is a one-stop-shop for performing multiple, simultaneous enrichments with different ontologies, in the B73 reference proteome and non-reference proteomes in the Nested Association Mapping (NAM) population. The ontologies not only focus on biological function (GO terms, Biological Pathway, Metabolic Function, Protein Domain), but also on uncovering trends in single-cell and subcellular spatial distribution, gene over-representation at specific loci, and agronomic traits for breeding efforts. FETA is also designed as a complement to popular transcriptomics pipelines (EdgeR, DESeq, limma, etc.), allowing users to upload their data (or use one of our pre-loaded datasets), visualize differentially expressed genes (DEGs), and directly perform functional enrichment on these subsets – all in one place.

FETA is hosted by the Maize Genetics and Genomics Database (MaizeGDB), the community database for maize genetics and genomics. Annotation data is drawn from curated sources at MaizeGDB, including GO terms, KEGG, Reactome, and others. Results can be exported as tables, PNG, or SVG for use in publications. Available Tools
B73 Enrichment Analysis Perform enrichments on B73 gene lists and visualize results as bar charts, dot plots, and concept networks. Supports various functional, spatial, loci, and trait ontologies. Non-B73 Enrichment Enrichment analyses on gene lists from non-reference genomes. GO term, pathway, and metabolic ontologies are currently supported.
RNA-Seq Analysis Use curated MaizeGDB datasets, or upload your own RNA-Sequencing data to view and perform functional enrichment on differentially expressed genes. Supports data/experiments on any of the NAM founders. Other Analytics Additional tools for gene set analysis beyond standard enrichment. Currently supports studying the distribution of gene evolutionary ages, and protein annotation assessments for B73 and NAM founder genomes.
